--- Aditya - The Driver

The captain of the chariot who defines the primary form of divine love operating in your life.

The Adityas are the sons of Aditi, whose name signifies "the undivided." She
represents the infinite, whole power that fuels the indivisible love emitted by
the Sun. Because the human mind cannot grasp this love in its totality, Aditi
gave birth to the twelve Adityas—specific, focused embodiments of her universal
love. Each Aditya takes a turn driving the chariot of the Sun throughout the
year, allowing a specific form of love to operate as a primary power in
creation. Every Aditya is accompanied by six other classes of beings who assist
in fulfilling its divine mission. 

--- Rishi - The Navigator

The primordial seer who provides the blueprint of divine law, keeping your life in harmony with reality.

The rishis are the primordial seers who perceived the fundamental workings of
Reality and transmitted this wisdom to humanity. They are the progenitors of the
various races of the world. By seeing into the very structure of existence,
their lives and teachings reflect the innate Laws that govern a harmonious and
enlivened creation. They represent the Law that allows life to function in
alignment with the divine.

--- Yaksha - The Anchor of Authenticity

The nature spirit who connects you to body and environment, allowing you to express your authentic individuality.

A yaksha is a nature spirit whose primary mission is to differentiate. The
yaksha understands that every being and object is unique, and seeks to express
that difference in every way possible. Their king is Kubera, the lord of wealth,
illustrating that true wealth and wellness come from being authentically
ourselves. The most prominent differentiator in our experience is the physical
body.

--- Rakshasa -  The Enforcer of Power

The protective force that challenges you to use your raw power wisely —
protection by destroying threats. Grow or suffer.

While often translated as "demons," rakshasas are not inherently malicious. The
name is derived from the root "raksh," which means "to protect." This protective
force is the true intent of the rakshasa, but it is a powerful energy that can
easily go astray. When it becomes disproportionate or misdirected, it becomes
"demonic." Thus, each rakshasa represents the struggle to use our power wisely
and protectively without it becoming a source of terror.

--- Gandharva - The Catalyst of Action

The celestial warrior and musician who inspires you to act, walk your true path, and express your destiny outward.

Gandharvas are celestial musicians and divine warriors. Music and battle serve
as metaphors for the ways our internal love and consciousness flow outward to
manifest in the physical realm. The gandharva force motivates us toward action
and inspires us to walk our true path, expressing our innate abilities in
service to the world. It represents the sacred masculine energy within us that
drives us toward the fulfillment of our individual destiny.

--- Apsara - The Mood Maker

The inner alchemist who sets the emotional tone and conditions of your mind — the catalyst for your potential to bear fruit.

Apsaras are celestial maidens representing the subtle, inner dimensions of the mind and
emotions. Traditionally, they are said to distract meditating rishis, drawing them back
toward the world. This force does not create anything itself; rather, it acts as a
catalyst. The state of our apsara force determines how our energy is directed and how
our latent potential actually bears fruit. This influence is primarily felt through our
emotions and the "conditions" of our inner life. The apsaras create the "mood" that is
necessary for something to happen.

--- Naga - The Evolutionary Pull

The deep unconscious life force that binds you to the material world but holds the doorway to spiritual release.

The nagas represent the primal life force that has accepted the illusion of
individual existence, often symbolized as a serpent coiled at the base of the
spine. Nagas possess an innate, unconscious drive to untie the knots of their
existence and reascend to their home in the crown. This force both binds us to
the material realm and serves as the very door through which we find release. It
is the deep, unconscious pull that eventually leads all beings back to their
spiritual source.
