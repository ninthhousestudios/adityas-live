--- parjanya | aditya

The Love of Life

We do not want to die because we love life so much — but this is not the love
exhibited by Parjanya. The Love of Humanity of Vivasvan Aditya, on the opposite
side of the circle, is love for humanity as a whole, not of any particular human
being; if one human must die or suffer for the good of all humanity, Vivasvan
can accept this.

Parjanya is similar but related to the whole web of Life, of all creation.
Parjanya's prime directive is that Life should flourish, Life itself as a whole;
but any particular form of life may not be saved in particular. The nature of
Life is to flow and move, like a river, which will demolish anything in its
path. Parjanya teaches us how to move with Life, not deny it and close ourselves
off to it.

=== Reflections

Do you love life? Do you love your life? Do you see life as one thing, or as
many separate things? Is Life itself fundamentally valuable, or does each kind
of life have its own value compared to all the others?

When you wake and see that it is raining outside, what is your reaction? "Go
away, come again another day"? Or perhaps you accept it: "it's raining but I'm
still alive and I'm grateful for that"? Or perhaps you enjoy the rain, knowing
how essential it is, and can appreciate it regardless of whether you want it to
be raining or not?

With what kind of rain do you water your life? With the rain of toxicity,
judgment, and negativity? Or with the rain of gratitude, acceptance, and
compassion?


--- bharadvaja | rishi

The Law of Return

Bharadvaja had a very difficult birth and home life. He was the child of
adultery, then got kicked out of the womb by the fetus already there, and then
he was raised among strangers. Yet, despite these difficulties, Bharadvaja was
able to transmute his difficult past into spiritual gold. He spent many
lifetimes focused on his spiritual quest.

The law of Bharadvaja is the Law of Return. Our true homes are in the infinite,
and we are all bound to return there. The question is not whether, but when, and
how much suffering we have to endure along the path.

=== Reflections

Do you believe there is something after this life? Do you believe there is
something beyond this life? Do you accept that you are the body and everything
that happens to you is real?

How do you deal with your wounds — your mental or emotional wounds? Do you seek
to understand them and thus transmute them? Or do you let them devour you,
always remembering and thinking about the bad things that happened?

Think of a time when you broke a bad habit, when you returned to yourself. How
did you do it? What was the motivation?


--- senajit | yaksha

The Body Transcended

Amma often gives darshan for hours at a time. On a few occasions, she has given
darshan for more than 20 hours continuously, sitting still in one spot as she
hugs and consoles a constant stream of thousands of people. Her body is not
immune to pain and suffering, but she is simply able to ignore it.

Amma knows who she really is — not the body, not the mind, but the supreme
reality. This means that for her the body is not important in itself — the body
is a vehicle through which she works, but does not bind her in any way. She has
transcended the needs of the body, allowing it to channel the divine truth.

=== Reflections

How do you treat your body? As a tool? As an enemy? As a lover? Do bodily
states distract you from other concerns, or are you able to ignore them when
necessary in order to focus on what needs your attention?

Is your body in a state of health and harmony, or disease and decay? Regardless,
does your body distract you — either due to its ill health or its beauty? How
focused are you on your body in life? Is it a primary concern, or not very
important?

Do you consider the body to be physical only, or do you feel that your mind and
emotions are equally part of your body?


--- varcas | rakshasa

The Struggle of Not Blocking the Flow

Ramana Maharshi and others say that Liberation is all that exists, and the
problem is that we forget — we put walls between it and us. The spiritual path
in this sense is entirely negative: it is about forgetting, letting go, breaking
down. Once all the false is removed, then what remains is only the truth. But
wouldn't it be better not to create false things in the first place?


=== Reflections

How often do you get in your own way? Everything would have gone just fine, if
only you had not done that one thing? How often does this happen to you?

Does your life force flow freely, or does it feel blocked? If it is blocked,
what by? Is it a knot in your back? If so, do you think the problem is
inherently physical, or could this physical manifestation be an indicator of
something deeper?

Have you felt the life force of another human being? Did it align with the
appearance of their body?


--- vishvavasu | gandharva

The Sacred Masculine's Sacred Feminine

Every force in creation has a complement, an inner counterpart that completes
it. Vishvavasu in Parjanya represents the masculine principle that has fully
embraced its feminine aspect — the emotions, desires, vulnerabilities, and
fluctuations that flow through us like water. Rather than armoring against these
inner currents, Vishvavasu moves with them, allowing their wisdom to inform his
actions in the world.

This is not weakness but completion. When the sacred masculine is connected to
its own sacred feminine, it can respond to life with the full range of its being
— strong yet flexible, directed yet receptive, like a river that carves the
landscape precisely because it yields to gravity.

=== Reflections

How connected are you to your feminine side — to your emotions, desires,
opinions, moods, and other fluctuations? Are you aware of what is vulnerable in
you? What do you do to protect it?

Do you enjoy changing your consciousness, either through practices or chemicals?
If so, why? What do you get out of it? Do you believe it is necessary, or are
you doing it for fun? Have these experiences helped you?

How often do other people see you cry?

--- visvaci | apsara

She Who Is Always in the Mood for What Is Happening Right Now

Life does not wait for us to be ready. It simply happens, and our task is to
meet it. Visvaci brings the capacity to be emotionally present for whatever is
unfolding in this moment — not wishing it were different, not clinging to what
came before, but fully engaging with what is.

This is not passive acceptance but an active embrace. Visvaci attunes us to the
truth that what appears as poison at first may become nectar, and what seems
like nectar may reveal itself as poison. By remaining present and emotionally
engaged, we are able to receive the full teaching of each moment as it arrives.

=== Reflections

How often do you wish whatever is happening weren't happening? Have you ever
gone into this feeling as it is happening and explored it? Were you able to come
to terms with your situation, to at least tolerate it? Or perhaps even accept
and begin to enjoy it, or see it as valuable?

Have you experienced that at first something seems like nectar, but eventually
it becomes poison? What about the opposite — something that at first seemed like
poison becomes truly helpful and nourishing to you?


--- airavata | naga

The Burning Desire for Liberation

Of all the nagas, Airavata represents the most intense evolutionary urge:
mumukshutva, the burning desire for spiritual liberation. This is a thirst that
no worldly pleasure can quench. It is the deepest current in the human soul,
pulling us toward the source from which all life emerged.

This desire is not born of despair but of recognition. Just as a person dying of
thirst is consumed by the need for water, Airavata instills in us a longing so
profound that all other pursuits pale in comparison. It is the final fuel for
the spiritual journey — the force that ensures we do not stop until we have
returned home.

=== Reflections

What do you have a burning desire for? How long has that desire been burning?
What is its fuel source? Do you feed it intentionally, or do you feel controlled
by it?

Have you ever been so thirsty that even the sight of water was a joy, not to
speak of actually drinking it? Have you ever felt a sudden coolness blow into
you, easing whatever distress was burning?

What do you enjoy in life? Are the things you enjoy more in the physical realm,
the mental realm, the emotional realm, or the spiritual realm?
