--- varuna | aditya

All-Encompassing Love

The sky and the ocean are the two most vast features of the natural world. They
are both connected and distinct—opposites that complement one another. Together,
they hold and witness everything in existence, providing the infinite space in
which life unfolds. They are impartial; the ocean rejects no one, and the rain
falls on everyone alike.

This vastness expresses the Love of Varuna. It is all-encompassing, regarding
everything as one and holding all of creation in a non-judgmental embrace.
Jupiter is exalted here, reflecting this sign's profound capacity for spiritual
realization.

However, Varuna is not merely "love and light." The ocean contains both beauty
and terrors, and Varuna accepts them all. For a god, this totality is natural;
for a human, it is an intense challenge. We often fluctuate between intense
love and intense judgment, or intense violence. The lesson of Varuna is to
simply accept everything as it is. All-encompassing love is our nature, but
judgment is the dam that blocks its flow.

=== Reflections

Where are you seeking your ultimate fulfillment?

How freely does love flow from you, and how openly do you receive it? Do you
keep a tally of love given and received, or is it a natural current that simply flows?

Can you see others as reflections of yourself? When you judge someone else's
flaws, can you find that same seed within your own heart?

How do you respond to what is "ugly" or "broken"? Can you find love for that
which you do not like?


--- vasishtha | rishi

The Law of Consciousness

Vasishtha is the chief of the Saptarshis, the seven mind-born sages of Brahma.
Though he was eligible for liberation and could have left the world behind, he
chose to remain as the guru to Lord Rama. His name means "the best" or "the most
wealthy."

His wealth was symbolized by the magical cow, Nandini, who could manifest
anything her owner desired. The warrior Vishvamitra repeatedly attacked
Vasishtha's hermitage to steal the cow, driven by lust and worldly power.
However, Nandini always produced exactly what was needed to repel the attacks.
The cow was sensitive to the intent of her owner; she served Vasishtha because
he used her gifts only for the good of others.

Vasishtha expounds the Law of Consciousness: the outer world is a reflection of
the inner state. Just as the Moon reflects the Sun, everything around you
reflects the true self within. Understanding and living by this law is the key
to opening the all-encompassing love of Varuna.

=== Reflections

Where do your thoughts naturally dwell? Are you able to intentionally redirect
your mind when it wanders into negativity?

Do you instinctively look for the good in a situation, or the bad? Is your first
reaction one of appreciation or criticism?

Are your daily routines—especially around waking and sleeping—intentional and
consistent, or are they haphazard?

Do you practice any form of mental training, such as meditation or chanting, to
help you inspect and adjust your state of consciousness?


--- rathachitra | yaksha

The Body Radiant

While the first three yakshas govern the creation, power, and feedback of the
body, Rathachitra governs the energetic vibrations it emits. These vibrations
are not inherent to the physical tissues themselves; their source is the state
of our consciousness, which is flowing out through the cells of our bodies.

"Chitra" means "excellent," "distinguished," or "clear." Through Rathachitra, a
healthy and open consciousness modulates the body's vibration, emitting a
radiance or "luster" that others can feel and sometimes even see. This radiance
has the power to affect the surrounding atmosphere. Conversely, a negative or
closed state of consciousness emits a heavy vibration that can repel others.
Those with a prominent Varuna influence are often powerful emitters of this energy.

=== Reflections

What does the word "luster" evoke in you? Would you describe yourself as having
it? What about "radiant"? Have you every felt radiant? Has anyone ever told you
that you were "glowing"? How did that feel?

Recall a time you encountered someone with a powerful, radiant presence. What
did that feel like? Have you ever noticed others having that reaction to you?

How do you feel upon waking? Do you feel vital and radiant, or do you feel a
heavy lack of energy?

What does your current state of "luster" reveal about how well your life's
actions are aligned with your true purpose?


--- chitrasvana | rakshasa

The Struggle of Bad Vibrations

While the yaksha of Varuna, Rathachitra, governs the emission of vibrations,
Chitrasvana governs the internal work of managing the vibrations themselves. To
emit a harmonious radiance, we must first hear and understand the "music" we
are playing within our own consciousness.

"Chitrasvana" refers to an "excellent sound." The human struggle is that we are
often constitutionally biased toward negativity, producing discordant
"vibrations" more easily than harmonious ones. The struggle of Chitrasvana is to
become aware of this inner music. Many people are unaware of how their internal
discordant notes—their moods and emotional states—affect themselves and everyone
around them.

Once we hear our own music clearly, we can begin to shift it. This requires
delving deep into our consciousness to understand the source of the discord. In
the realm of Varuna, we transform these sounds through unconditional love.
Loving our own inner "shadows" creates the most harmonious resonance we can
offer the world.

=== Reflections

What kind of "music" are you currently broadcasting into the world?

How closely do you listen to your own internal emotional state? Do you have
practices—like journaling or nature walks—that help you hear your inner sounds
more clearly?

Do you notice the "vibrations" emitted by others? Is your own resonance more
similar to those you find beautiful or those you find discordant?

Can you identify a time when you transformed a negative inner sound into a
beautiful one? What are you working to harmonize right now?


--- huhu | gandharva

He Who Can Only Say No but Does So Perfectly

Huhu is the brother of Haha (the gandharva of Mitra). While Haha finds what
works and says "yes," Huhu understands what is not right for us and says "no."
The love of Varuna is all-encompassing, but Huhu teaches us that loving
everything does not mean accepting everything into our personal sphere.

The "no" of Huhu is not a rejection of something as "bad," but a recognition
that it is not in alignment with our individual path. This discernment comes
through introspection and a deep connection to one's own consciousness.

When we are too focused on external demands, we may use Huhu to satisfy others
rather than ourselves. An afflicted Huhu might also lead us to judge others,
assuming that because something is wrong for us, it must be wrong for everyone.
The proper use of Huhu is to diligently enforce the boundaries that keep our
lives in their appropriate places, listening to the inner wisdom of the soul.

=== Reflections

How connected are you to your own "no"? When it is time to set a boundary, can
you do so powerfully and with clarity?

Are you able to say "no" to a situation without judging it as "evil" or "wrong"
for others?

Which of your "no"s were ignored or disrespected when you were a child? How are
you working to reclaim your right to choose what is in alignment for you?


--- sahajanya | apsara

She who makes us powerful in our own skin

We are all born with innate power, but this is not always obvious. It is not
always expressed because many layers cover up this potential. Some people
naturally radiate this power and you can even tell. Some people feel like being
themselves all the time. They just can't not be who they are and this shines
from them like sunlight. And some people don't know who they are and thus cannot
feel like being themselves. Sahajanya means "born with". We are all born with
ability to be ourselves. Sahanjanya helps us get into the mood to claim it.


=== Reflections

Do you mostly do what is natural to you? Or not? Do you even know what is
natural to you? Have you ever investigated to find out what is natural to you,
or if what you think is natural to you is truly natural to you?

Are you full of your innate power? What parts of your "natural state"—your
looks, your talents, your birth—are you still trying to "improve" or hide? Are
you using your "mighty" energy to manipulate others, or are you allowing your
innate grace to inspire them?

Who in your life makes you feel "okay with yourself" just by being in their
presence? What is the quality of their "natural state"?

Consider what innate power looks like. Think about whether you have ever
encountered someone with a strong Sahajanya force, who causes everyone to turn
their heads just by their presence.

--- shukra | naga

The Grace of God

The Sun represents the universal soul, yet the Aditya ruled by the Sun (Indra)
relates to the personal spiritual path. The Moon represents our individual
consciousness, yet the Aditya ruled by the Moon (Varuna) is the seat of
spiritual liberation and the exaltation of Jupiter. Why is this so?

The reason lies in the nature of the mind. The individual mind—represented by
the Moon—often forgets that it has no light of its own and merely reflects the
Sun. It wrongly assumes its light is its own. This is the central problem of the
spiritual path, and the solution resides in Varuna.

True transformation requires three factors: time, effort, and divine grace.
While we have control over our effort, we cannot force the dawning of
all-encompassing love. We can work to harmonize our vibrations, but we never
know when that effort will bear fruit. Shukra represents the moment when the
effort ends and Grace takes over. It cannot be bribed or commanded; we must
simply do the work and wait for the moment of clarity when the truth
spontaneously dawns.

=== Reflections

How much have you truly changed over your lifetime? Are you the same person you
were as a child, or has your essence shifted?

Has a truth ever spontaneously "dawned" on you? A moment where you didn't
understand, and then suddenly, without effort, you did?

Have you ever experienced a sudden light in a moment of total darkness—a way out
that appeared just when you thought all was lost?
